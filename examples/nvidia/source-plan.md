# NVIDIA source plan

Sources are selected by the role they play in a research question. Authority is contextual, not a universal ranking.

| Priority | Source group | Role | Minimum Phase 1 material |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| P0 | SEC / regulatory filings | Reported financial and risk facts | Matching 10-Q or 10-K |
| P0 | NVIDIA Investor Relations | Release, presentation, and formal guidance | Earnings release and presentation |
| P0 | Earnings call | Management explanation, forecasts, and analyst concerns | Transcript or authorized recording |
| P1 | Pre-earnings expectations | Market expectation snapshot | Timestamped consensus or clearly attributed estimates |
| P1 | Industry context | Independent checks on demand, supply, and constraints | Small, question-driven evidence set |
| P1 | Internal research | Institution-specific expectations and thesis | Prior model or research note when available |
| P2 | Market and news data | Event timing and market context | Event-window data with provider metadata |

## Collection rules

- Freeze the earnings cycle before collection.
- Prefer original issuer or regulator artifacts over reposted copies.
- Capture pre-earnings estimates before they can be revised.
- Record paywall and license constraints; do not commit restricted documents.
- A source plan item may remain missing. Coverage reporting must expose the gap.
- Industry sources are added only for an explicit thesis or verification question.

## Initial verification questions

The first cycle should be able to test:

1. Do the filing, earnings release, and presentation agree on selected reported metrics?
2. Were consensus snapshots taken before the earnings release?
3. Which management statements are forecasts rather than reported facts?
4. Which forecast differences are explained by definition, period, or assumptions?
5. What evidence would weaken the selected thesis rather than merely support it?
